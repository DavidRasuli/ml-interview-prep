🎯 ML Deployment Scenarios

Practice real-world deployment challenges. Work through these scenarios to build practical understanding of ML infrastructure decisions, tradeoffs, and problem-solving approaches.

Global Assumptions

  • Cloud Platform: AWS only (focusing on AWS-specific instance types and services)
  • Model Type: Large Language Models (LLMs) only - no computer vision or other model types
  • Production Context: All scenarios assume production deployments with real users and business constraints

Core Deployment Scenarios

Essential scenarios covering the fundamentals of ML deployment

1
Greenfield Deployment
Beginner
Your company is launching a new chatbot feature and needs to deploy a 7B parameter LLM from HuggingFace. There's no existing ML infrastructure - you're starting from scratch. The product team expects to handle around 100 concurrent users initially, with typical chatbot usage patterns (bursts of activity, then quiet periods).

Key Questions to Consider:

  • Which AWS instance type should you choose and why?
  • What precision format makes sense to start with?
  • Should you optimize the model immediately or deploy the base version first?
  • How do you validate the deployment is working correctly?
  • What monitoring do you need from day one?
2
Model Upgrade (Same Infrastructure)
Beginner
Your chatbot has been running for 2 months with a 7B parameter model on g5 instances. User feedback shows the quality isn't great - responses are sometimes irrelevant or incomplete. You've found a newer, better-performing 7B model on HuggingFace with significantly improved benchmarks. It should fit on your existing hardware.

Key Questions to Consider:

  • How do you safely deploy the new model without disrupting users?
  • What's your A/B testing strategy?
  • How do you measure if the new model is actually better in production?
  • What could go wrong during the swap?
  • How do you rollback if users prefer the old model?
3
Model Upgrade (Needs Better Hardware)
Intermediate
After A/B testing, users clearly prefer higher-quality responses. You want to upgrade from your current 7B model to a 13B model for better accuracy. The problem: your 13B model won't fit in the 24GB VRAM on your current g5 instances. The larger model also has higher latency requirements.

Key Questions to Consider:

  • What are your hardware upgrade options (instance types, multi-GPU, etc.)?
  • How do you estimate the cost increase?
  • How do you justify the additional cost to stakeholders?
  • What's your migration path from g5 to the new instances?
  • Could optimization techniques (quantization, etc.) let you stay on existing hardware?

Performance & Optimization Scenarios

Scenarios focused on improving performance, reducing latency, and managing costs

4
Multi-Language Performance Optimization
Intermediate
Your Python-based serving layer (FastAPI) is handling requests, but you're stuck at around 100 requests per second. Product needs you to scale to 1,000 req/s to support a major marketing campaign. The bottleneck isn't the model inference itself (which runs in C++ via ONNX Runtime) - it's the Python request handling, preprocessing, and postprocessing logic.

Key Questions to Consider:

  • When would you use Go vs Rust vs C++ for the serving layer?
  • What parts should stay in Python and what should be rewritten?
  • How does Python's GIL factor into this decision?
  • Could async/await or multiprocessing solve this without changing languages?
  • What are the tradeoffs in development time vs performance gains?
5
Budget Constraints / Cost Optimization
Advanced
Finance has mandated a 50% infrastructure cost reduction within 2 months. Your current setup: a 70B parameter model running on expensive g5.48xlarge instances serving 5,000 req/s. Meanwhile, users are already complaining about 300ms average latency. You need to cut costs dramatically while maintaining or improving user experience.

Key Questions to Consider:

  • Should you use quantization (FP16→INT8/INT4)? What are the accuracy tradeoffs?
  • Should you use model distillation to move from 70B to a 13B or 7B model?
  • Could switching to cheaper instance types work? Which ones?
  • What about a hybrid approach - small model for simple queries, large model for complex ones?
  • How do you measure and communicate the tradeoffs to stakeholders?
6
Latency Crisis
Intermediate
Your LLM deployment currently averages 200ms response time. Product just informed you that they need to get this down to under 50ms for a new real-time feature launching in 3 weeks. You cannot change the model itself (business requirement - it's fine-tuned and performs well). You need to make the infrastructure faster.

Key Questions to Consider:

  • What optimization levers can you pull without changing the model?
  • Would request batching help or hurt latency?
  • Should you use TensorRT optimization? What precision changes make sense?
  • Could better instance types with faster GPUs solve this?
  • What about model warm-up and caching strategies?

Scale & Reliability Scenarios

Scenarios dealing with traffic spikes, multi-region deployments, and production issues

7
Scaling for Viral Traffic
Intermediate
Your chatbot feature is handling 1,000 req/s comfortably. Tonight at 5 PM, a celebrity will tweet about your product and you expect traffic to spike to 10,000-15,000 req/s within hours. Your infrastructure team needs a plan by end of day. Budget is flexible for this emergency, but you need to scale quickly and reliably.

Key Questions to Consider:

  • How do you scale quickly (within hours)?
  • What's your auto-scaling strategy?
  • How do you handle load balancing across multiple instances?
  • What batching strategies would help maximize throughput?
  • What could go wrong and how do you prepare for failures?
8
Multi-Region Deployment
Advanced
Your product serves customers globally. Currently everything runs in us-east-1. US users get 50ms response times, but European users are seeing 300ms+ latency and complaining. You need to deploy in EU regions while managing costs, model versioning, and maintaining consistency across regions.

Key Questions to Consider:

  • Which AWS regions should you deploy to and why?
  • How do you handle model updates across multiple regions?
  • What's your strategy for routing users to the nearest region?
  • How do you keep costs reasonable with multi-region deployment?
  • What happens if one region fails?
9
Model Drift / Quality Issues
Intermediate
Your model has been running smoothly for two weeks. Suddenly, users start reporting that responses are getting worse - more irrelevant answers, occasional gibberish, and lower satisfaction scores. Your monitoring shows accuracy metrics dropping from 85% to 72% over the past 3 days. Nothing has changed in your deployment configuration.

Key Questions to Consider:

  • What's your systematic debugging process?
  • Could this be a data distribution shift in user queries?
  • How do you check if the model itself has changed (model versioning)?
  • What monitoring and logging should you have in place to catch this earlier?
  • How do you communicate this issue to stakeholders while investigating?
10
Security & Compliance Constraints
Advanced
Your legal team has just informed you that due to new regulations, your LLM must run in a private VPC with no internet access - all data must stay within the VPC for compliance. However, your current architecture relies on the model calling an external knowledge base API for retrieval-augmented generation (RAG). Users need this RAG functionality to continue working.

Key Questions to Consider:

  • How do you architect this within the private VPC constraint?
  • Do you need to bring the knowledge base into the VPC?
  • What about VPC endpoints or private links?
  • How do you handle model updates and monitoring without internet access?
  • What are the performance and cost implications?
